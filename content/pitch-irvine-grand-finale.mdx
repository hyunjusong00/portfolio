  The Civic Center was already full when I got there. Even the back of the room where people were standing was packed. Honestly, that's what a healthy startup scene looks like to me. Not just the pitch itself, but the room that shows up for it.

  </div>
</div>

<div className="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-8 md:gap-10 items-center my-10">
  <div>

  The pitches that worked felt less like presentations and more like conversations. The founder wasn't really performing. They were just explaining. I could follow the story of why this, why now, why us, without having to translate any "founder language" in my head first.

  </div>
  <img
    src="/IMG_2830.jpg"
    alt="A founder mid-pitch on the Grand Finale stage"
    className="rounded-xl w-full aspect-[4/3] object-cover border border-border !my-0"
  />
</div>

<div className="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-8 md:gap-10 items-center my-10">
  <img
    src="/IMG_2837.jpg"
    alt="The room watching a pitch"
    className="rounded-xl w-full aspect-[4/3] object-cover border border-border !my-0"
  />
  <div>

  Five minutes isn't really a constraint. It's more of a forcing function. The pitches that struggled weren't bad ideas. They just hadn't been compressed enough yet. If the room doesn't lean in within the first 30 seconds, no slide deck is going to save you after.

  </div>
</div>

<div className="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-8 md:gap-10 items-center my-10">
  <div>

  I work on **[Trywepp](#projects)**, an AI platform that connects VCs with early-stage founders. So tonight was meta for me. The product I'm building runs on the same thing I saw play out in the room. A founder usually has more signal to share than their deck shows. Watching it live was a useful reminder that a tool doesn't replace the discipline of pitching. It just gives founders another medium to do it in. The ones who can pitch in a room can also pitch on a page. The product just has to leave space for that.

  </div>

Thanks to Irvine Tech Day and the City of Irvine for putting this together. It's nice to have a place where local founders can just show up as themselves. Already looking forward to the next one.
