## Clarity is a superpower

<div className="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-8 md:gap-10 items-center my-10">
  <div>

  The best pitches felt less like presentations and more like conversations. The founder didn't perform — they explained. You could follow the thread of *why this, why now, why us* without having to translate from "founder language" first.

  </div>
  <img
    src="/IMG_2830.jpg"
    alt="A founder mid-pitch on the Grand Finale stage"
    className="rounded-xl w-full aspect-[4/3] object-cover border border-border !my-0"
  />
</div>

<div className="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-8 md:gap-10 items-center my-10">
  <img
    src="/IMG_2837.jpg"
    alt="The room watching a pitch"
    className="rounded-xl w-full aspect-[4/3] object-cover border border-border !my-0"
  />
  <div>

  Five minutes is not a constraint. It's a forcing function. The pitches that struggled weren't bad ideas — they were ideas that hadn't been compressed yet. If you can't get the room to lean in within the first 30 seconds, the deck doesn't save you.

  </div>
</div>

## Why I'll be back next time

<div className="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-8 md:gap-10 items-center my-10">
  <div>

  I work on **[Trywepp](#projects)**, an AI platform connecting VCs with early-stage founders. The product is built on the same observation Pitch Irvine made tangible tonight: the signal a founder *can* communicate runs ahead of the deck that's supposed to package it. Watching the live version is a useful correction — what a tool replaces is *the medium*, not *the discipline*. The founders who pitched well in person will pitch well on a webpage too. The product just has to give them room to do it.
