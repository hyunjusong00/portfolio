## The thing that broke my brain

What surprised me most wasn't the physical sets — it was that the studio doesn't always need them anymore.

They showed us a virtual production stage where the entire set can be replaced from one location. You point a camera at a real spot and the system composites in a fully built environment in real time, photo-realistically — background, lighting, depth, all moving with the camera. A subway station you've never been to. An office on the 30th floor that doesn't exist. A version of New York that lives only as data.

## Why I keep thinking about it

  I build AI products. The conversation around AI in creative industries is usually about what gets *replaced* — actors, illustrators, writers, set builders. What I saw at LA Center Studios was different. The physical sets weren't being thrown out. They were sitting right next to the virtual stage. Both being used. Both being respected.

  </div>
</div>

That's the part I want to remember: the studios most prepared for AI aren't the ones replacing craft. They're the ones who know it well enough to extend it.
